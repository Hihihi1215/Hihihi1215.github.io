Spiderman: No way Home



                                Synopsis

With Spider-identity Man's revealed, our friendly neighbourhood web-slinger is no longer able to separate his regular life as Peter Parker from the high stakes of being a superhero. When Peter seeks assistance from Doctor Strange, the stakes rise even higher, forcing him to discover what it truly means to be Spider-Man.


                                Directors

Director: Jon Watts
Genre: Action
Production companies: Columbia Pictures, Marvel Studios, Pascal Pictures


                                Review

"No Way Home" is a distinguished and entertaining film that is easily one of the best movies of 2021. The action sequences are tense, and the cinematography is stunning–particularly when the audience sees three different Spider-Man battling together for the first time. The acting is tremendous.






A Star is Born



                                Synopsis

Jackson Maine, a seasoned musician, meets and falls in love with struggling artist Ally. She has almost given up on her dream of becoming a famous singer until Jackson coaxes her into the spotlight. Even as Ally's career soars, the personal side of their relationship is deteriorating, as Jackson battles his own internal demons.


                                Directors

Director: Bradley Cooper
Distributed by: Warner Bros. Pictures, SF Studios
Genre: Music/romance


                                Review

"A Star Is Born" is such a great Hollywood myth that it's no wonder Hollywood keeps telling it. Whatever the era, the director or the headliners, it relates the story of two lovers on dramatically differing paths: a famous man who’s furiously racing to the bottom (Bradley Cooper in this movie) and a woman (Lady Gaga) who’s soaring to the top. This latest and fourth version is a gorgeous heartbreaker (bring tissues). Like its finest antecedents, it wrings tears from its romance and thrills from a steadfast belief in old-fashioned, big-feeling cinema. That it’s also a perverse fantasy about men, women, love and sacrifice makes it all the better.

                                Directors

Director: Jia Ling
Distributed by: Alibaba Pictures
Genre: Comedy


                                Review

this is an imaginative and beautiful drama with comedy that is sentimentally warm and touching. the story is based on the director's relationship with her mother, and is about a personal reconciliation with regret/grief, love, redemption, and filial piety. the director also acts in this film as the daughter. it has a beautiful story and plot that are well thought out, well written, well planned/controlled/executed with mature/ripe preparations. the humor tends to be light, silly humor through the play of words, and sometimes infused with exaggerated/corny dialogues and nonverbals. there is a very close relationship between the mother and daughter. the mother not wanting her daughter to be looked down by their relatives, has a certain expectation of her daughter. however, before the daughter could realize the expectation, an accident occurs, that transports the daughter back in time. in this new reality, the daughter gets to acquaint herself with the younger version of her mother. the daughter decides to reconciles with her regret/grief, and to redeem herself by trying to make her mother's more pleasant. but, the whole pilgrimage has a surprising twist.
